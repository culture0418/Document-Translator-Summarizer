SISTER SHIP OF OLYMPICThe Titanic, largest liner in commission, was a sister ship of the Olympic.The registered tonnage of each vessel is estimated as 45,000, but officers ofthe White Star Line say that the Titanic measured 45,328 tons. The Titanicwas commanded by Captain E. J. Smith, the White Star admiral, who hadpreviously been on the Olympic.She was 882 1/2 long, or about four city blocks, and was 5000 tons biggerthan a battleship twice as large as the dreadnought Delaware.
